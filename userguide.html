Overview:

The Xbox Series X is a powerful gaming console developed by Microsoft, delivering 4K resolution, up to 120 FPS, and lightning-fast performance. This guide will walk you through the initial setup process, ensuring you get started quickly and enjoy the best gaming experience possible.

Step #1:

Locate the Ports

On the back of the Xbox Series X, you’ll find:
HDMI Out – for connecting to your TV or monitor
Power Port – for plugging in the power cable

Connect the HDMI Cable

Plug one end of the HDMI cable into the HDMI OUT port on your Xbox.
Plug the other end into an HDMI 2.1 port on your TV or monitor (if available) for best performance.

Connect the Power Cable

Plug the power cord into the Power Port on your Xbox.
Connect the other end to a wall outlet or surge protector.

Turn On Your TV & Select the HDMI Input

see your TV remote to switch to the correct HDMI input where the Xbox is connected.

Step #2:

Locate the Power Button

The power button is located on the front of the console, in the upper-right corner.
It features the Xbox logo, which will light up when powered on.

Turn On the Console

Press the power button once. You should hear the startup chime, and the Xbox logo will illuminate.
If the console doesn't turn on, ensure the power cable is properly connected to both the Xbox and the power outlet.

Step #3:

Once your Xbox Series X is powered on, you’ll see an on-screen prompt to set up your console. You have two options:

Option 1: Use the Xbox Mobile App (Recommended)

Download the Xbox App On your smartphone, download the Xbox app from the App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android). Sign in with your Microsoft Account Use the same Microsoft account you plan to use on your Xbox. Follow On-Screen Instructions Enter the setup code displayed on your Xbox screen. Customize your settings and preferences directly from your phone.

Option 2: Set Up Directly on the Console

Use Your Controller Follow the on-screen prompts using your Xbox controller. Select Language & Location Choose your preferred language and region. Connect to the Internet Choose Wi-Fi or wired (Ethernet) connection for online features. Sign In or Create a Microsoft Account Log in to access Xbox Live, Game Pass, and cloud saves. Once setup is complete, your console will check for updates and prepare for gaming!

Step #4:

Insert AA Batteries

Open the battery compartment on the back of your Xbox Wireless Controller. Insert 2 AA batteries into the compartment, making sure to align the positive (+) and negative (-) terminals correctly.

Turn on the Controller

Press the Xbox button on the front of the controller to turn it on. The button will light up, indicating the controller is powered on.

Pair the Controller with the Console

If your controller isn’t already paired with your Xbox Series X, follow these steps:
Press and hold the Pair button on the back of the controller (located between the bumpers). The Xbox button on the controller will start blinking.
Press and hold the Pair button on the front of the Xbox Series X (next to the USB port) until the console’s Xbox logo begins to blink.
When the Xbox button on the controller stays solid, pairing is complete, and your controller is ready to use.
